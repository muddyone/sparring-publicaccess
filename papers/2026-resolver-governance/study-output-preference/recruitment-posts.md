## Blinding discipline baked into these posts (per pre-reg + 2026-06-04 amendment)

- The posts **do not name SPARRING**. Neither does the sign-up landing page (the "AI Decision-Evaluation Pilot"). The framework's name, the public repo, and which anonymized answer was which are disclosed **in-tool, only after a rater submits** (the post-submission reveal panel) — so reading the methodology can't bias the rating.
- Prior familiarity with the framework is **not** a sign-up gate. It is recorded as a graded covariate after submission and handled in analysis (§8h). Do not add a "have you heard of SPARRING?" screening question to recruitment.
- The GitHub repo link is **not** in the posts (the repo's `condition-{a-baseline,b-spar}/` dirs label the X/Y mapping). It is shared post-submission.

## Recruitment graphic (Option B — "too close to call")

_Added 2026-06-06. Visual hook to accompany both posts on Facebook + LinkedIn so the invitations aren't text-only. Direction chosen by BN: the **curiosity/challenge lane** — touch the intrinsic reason a person participates (the thrill of "where would *I* land?"), without manufacturing one. Produced via a Calla→(Zoe Kimura + Rosetta) ceremony; converged._

**Pre-reg / discipline note.** A recruitment graphic is recruitment material and carries the same constraints as the posts above. The hook was vetted against three disciplines and rejected two tempting-but-biasing angles in the process:

- **Anti-bias (rejected angles):** "Would you like to be part of an emerging AI methodology?" was rejected — it induces demand characteristics (an invested participant is no longer a naive judge), self-selects AI-enthusiasts, and overclaims SPARRING's value (a testable hypothesis, not an established movement to join). "Have you ever wondered how AI is built?" was rejected as expectation-mismatch (raters score memos; they don't learn AI internals).
- **No answer key (honest-claim):** the chosen hook elicits *your judgment vs. the machine's*, never *you vs. a correct answer* — there is no ground truth the rater is graded against; the human ratings **are** the data. Safe verbs: see / land / agree (never pick / find / get-right). "Too close to call" is the honest shorthand for the thin descriptive result (5.88 vs 5.79); "tie"/"=" was rejected as overstating it.
- **Blind integrity is a visual constraint (Zoe):** No SPARRING name, no repo/GitHub/monospace-terminal motif, no boxing-glove/"sparring" pun, no trophy/medal/winner imagery, and **no color or treatment that codes one answer-card over the other** (any brand color, border, or placeholder shading is applied to *both* cards identically, never one). _Correction (2026-06-06, on review of the rendered art): an earlier draft of this note claimed the two cards must not sit on a left/right axis, on the theory that the live tool randomizes X/Y position to protect the mapping. Zoe retired that on her own judgment — these cards are **blank placeholders** with no answer text, no labels, and no position→condition correspondence, so a side-by-side arrangement leaks nothing about the X/Y→condition mapping. The cards are kept upright and parallel (not tilted inward) for a **tone** reason only: to avoid an "A vs B / pick-the-winner" contest read that would fight the no-answer-key framing._

**Locked copy:**

> **Headline:** The machines called it too close to call. Where do you land?
> **Subline:** One look. That's it.

**Visual direction (as locked in the approved art):** ResourceForge brand palette — a deep **burgundy `#550202`** top band carrying the white headline; **off-white `#f5f5f2`** field; charcoal **`#1a1a1a`** subline; Montserrat type. Two blank answer-cards **side by side, upright and parallel** with even spacing (not tilted — see the tone note above), each with an **identical thin burgundy border** and identical **medium-grey placeholder lines** inside (visible but unreadable — abstract, not real words; the same on both cards). A simple round **non-code burgundy bot glyph** centered equidistant between the cards (neutral, favors neither). An empty **burgundy-outlined "your turn" speech bubble** below, tail pointing straight down (toward neither card). A small, secondary **"Participation Request"** corner tag (off-white pill, burgundy text) flags the post as a participation ask vs. one of BN's usual essays — graphic-only, a quiet category signal kept subordinate to the hook (Calla→Rosetta single-tag decision; Rosetta's register check landed on "Participation Request" as the shared tag wrong on neither platform). Calm, neutral — not a contest or scoreboard.
