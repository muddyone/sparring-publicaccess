## Final evaluation

**Substantively converged on (both agents agree)**:

- **Vocabulary**: Option 2 ("team members") as the canonical handbook address term, NOT "employees" (industry-default but external-voice-mismatched) and NOT "colleagues" (structurally conflicted, peer-register without peer-structure backing).
- **Hybrid architecture**: Use "employee" as the operative term in legally-load-bearing sections; "team member" elsewhere.
- **Clarifying front-matter clause** required, with explicit linkage to the at-will employment relationship (not merely terminology equivalence).
- **Section 7 savings clauses** required as architecture in rule-sections that interact with NLRA Section 7 protected concerted activity.

**Unresolved at cap (returns to partner adjudication)**:

1. **Hybrid-boundary completeness.** Generator enumerated 10 sections; Challenger holds that an additional 8+ legally-prescribed-vocabulary surfaces (I-9 / IRCA, USERRA, ERISA SPDs, OSHA recordkeeping, IRC payroll, SOX/Dodd-Frank whistleblower, non-FMLA leaves including PWFA / jury / military, harassment-investigation confidentiality) must be inside the boundary, OR a residual catch-all clause must be added: "any section implementing a federal or state statutory regime that uses 'employee' as its operative term."
2. **Front-matter prominence beyond placement and repetition.** Generator specified placement and cross-reference; Challenger holds that *Woolley*-line cases require typographic conspicuousness (bold / capitalized / bordered typography), a separate signature line on the acknowledgment page, and verbatim disclaimer repetition at cross-reference sites rather than mere cross-referencing.
3. **Section 7 architecture beyond five rule-section types.** Generator enumerated five (confidentiality, civility, social media, solicitation, concerted-activity-adjacent); Challenger holds that recording/photography (*Whole Foods*; GC 23-08), off-duty access (*Tri-County Medical*), investigation-confidentiality during pendency, dress code and insignia (*Home Depot USA* 2024), electronic communications (*Purple Communications*), and severance/arbitration carve-outs (*McLaren Macomb*) all require savings-clause architecture.

The disagreements are about completeness of the implementation enumeration, not about the underlying vocabulary or architectural choices. None of the Challenger's residuals invalidates the converged substance; each maps to a specific external authority where deficient language has been litigated or invalidated. A reasonable third iteration -- were one available -- would likely close all three by accepting the residual catch-all clause, the typographic-conspicuousness specification, and the expanded Section 7 enumeration.

## Recommendation for parent

**Substantive recommendation, both agents agree**: Adopt **Option 2 ("team members")** as TerraClarity's canonical handbook address term, NOT "employees" and NOT "colleagues." Implement a narrow hybrid: use "employee" as the operative term in legally-load-bearing sections (the Challenger's expanded enumeration is recommended over the Generator's 10-section list); use "team member" elsewhere. Include a clarifying front-matter clause (drafted text in Round 1 Challenger output) that explicitly integrates terminology equivalence with the at-will employment relationship, not merely terminology equivalence alone. Include Section 7 savings clauses as required architecture in all rule sections that interact with NLRA-protected concerted activity.

**Three implementation choices for partner adjudication** (the unresolved residuals):

1. Adopt the residual catch-all clause -- "any section implementing a federal or state statutory regime that uses 'employee' as its operative term" -- to cover the Challenger's identified omissions (I-9, USERRA, ERISA SPDs, OSHA, IRC payroll, SOX/Dodd-Frank, non-FMLA leaves, harassment-investigation confidentiality) without exhaustive enumeration. Recommended.
2. Adopt typographic-conspicuousness specifications for the front-matter clause: bold / capitalized / bordered typography distinct from body text; separate signature line on acknowledgment page; disclaimer language repeated verbatim at each cross-reference site. Strongly recommended for any state with active *Toussaint*-line jurisprudence (NJ, MI, NY, MN, IL, WA among others).
3. Expand Section 7 savings architecture beyond the Generator's five categories to include recording/photography rules, off-duty access, investigation-confidentiality demands during pendency, dress-code and insignia rules, electronic-communications policies, and severance/arbitration carve-outs (per *Home Depot USA* 2024, *Whole Foods*, *Purple Communications*, *Tri-County Medical*, and *McLaren Macomb*). Recommended; the Section 7 environment is post-*Stericycle* and the omissions carry real exposure.

The Challenger's signal of disagreement at iteration cap is structural, not theatrical: each unresolved residual maps to a specific external authority where the gap has been litigated. The partner (TerraClarity's leadership, with employment counsel) should treat the three residuals as scoping decisions, not optional polish. The vocabulary choice itself is settled; the implementation architecture has three open structural questions that benefit from explicit decision rather than inferred resolution.

Both agents agree that the recommendation does NOT include adopting "colleagues" (Option 3): the Generator's structural conflict-of-interest concern (the body whose decisions produce non-peer outcomes is advocating peer-register language) and the Challenger's *Clackamas*-adjacent ERISA misclassification-evidence concern both point the same direction. The partnership compensation committee chair's preference is acknowledged but does not survive cross-disciplinary scrutiny.
